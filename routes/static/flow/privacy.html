Surveillance

This is a surveillance guide, not a privacy policy. To see Ceccun Wi-Fi's privacy policy, visit the link at the bottom of the page. As you are on a Ceccun network, you are subject to Ceccun's privacy policy and governing of data control. Use of internet services are monitored for security and safety purposes.

You automatically give consent to Ceccun's privacy policy when you use Ceccun Wi-Fi.

What Is Monitored?

During your visit, Ceccun Wi-Fi will log and monitor certain aspects of your browsing experience. A full list is outlined below:

  1. The domain names of the websites you or your device visits, accesses or contacts. (via DNS)
  2. Names of the devices you use with Ceccun Wi-Fi (via your input)
  3. The IP addresses of the devices you use with Ceccun Wi-Fi (via DHCP)
  4. Mac addresses of the devices you use with Ceccun Wi-Fi (via DHCP)
  5. Mac addresses of the devices you don't use with Ceccun Wi-Fi (via Wi-Fi roaming)
  6. Exact location of your devices within the building at 5m accuracy (via triangulation)
  7. Exact location of you within the building at 10m accuracy (via your devices)
  8. The failure rate of this service on your device (via DHCP)
  9. The impact of failure on this service on your device (via DNS)

Reasons For Collection

Respectively, these are the reasons why Ceccun uses the data outlined above.

  1. To connect you to websites your device is requesting.
    To provide you with parental control tools.
    To provide you with a history of your browsing activity.
  2. To organise your devices in a way that makes sense to you in your account settings.
    To associate your devices with your account.
    To associate your history with your devices.
  3. To provide your devices with a way to connect to the internet.
    To assess whether your device is authenticated correctly.
    To assess whether your device is authorised to use this service.
  4. To allow your device to be connected to Ceccun Wi-Fi.
    To associate your device with a mac address.
    To ensure your device is not impersonated.
    To ensure equipment on the network can expect your device.
  5. To estimate the level of interference that devices not connected to Ceccun Wi-Fi have on the service.
    To conduct a census on how many people, places and devices are in the building, or on the street.
    To improve the accuracy of Ceccun Location Services for that device.
  6. To be able to seamlessly transfer your connection over to other equipment.
    To allow you to roam around the building without having to reconnect.
    To balance the load of the network across access points.
  7. To conduct a census on how many people are within the building or on the street.
  8. To ensure that the service is working correctly.
    To assess the cause of failure and the moments that led up to it.
    To improve the network for you and others.
  9. To ensure that future failures minimise or mitigate on disruption.
